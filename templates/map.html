Historical Accounts

Anguilla never developed a profitable plantation economy -- poor, dry soil kept it the "poor stepchild" of the sugar islands -- so it drew far less colonial attention, and far less of the surviving paper trail, than its wealthier neighbors. These first-hand accounts and testimonials were dug out via a wide research pass, each individually verified.
